Soy Foods (tofu, tempeh, edamame)

A useful protein-and-fiber food swap. Modestly lowers LDL and helps replace red/processed meat without losing protein.

Bottom Line

Soy foods are a good longevity food when they replace worse proteins. Choose tofu, tempeh, edamame, unsweetened soy milk. Do not infer the same from highly processed soy junk food.

What this means for you

Soy foods are not magic and not endocrine poison. Tofu, tempeh, edamame, and soy milk provide high-quality plant protein, fiber/isoflavones, and a practical way to replace red or processed meat. The LDL effect is modest; the food-swap value is larger.

How to actually do this

  • Use tofu/tempeh/edamame 2-5 times per week as a protein default.
  • Choose unsweetened soy milk if using milk alternatives; it has better protein than almond/oat milk.
  • Replace processed meat or cheese-heavy meals, not vegetables.
  • If on levothyroxine, keep dosing consistent and separate from soy-heavy meals by several hours.
Rough cost: $5-20/month depending on use. Tofu/edamame are usually cheap proteins.

Worth considering if

People reducing red meat, people wanting plant protein, people with elevated LDL/ApoB who need practical swaps.

Probably skip if

Soy allergy. People on highly restricted thyroid medication timing should separate soy from levothyroxine dosing.

Helps with

Heart disease Diabetes / metabolic Muscle loss (sarcopenia)

Evidence Summary

Meta-analyses of soy protein trials show modest LDL reductions, and cohort studies generally associate soy foods with neutral-to-beneficial cardiometabolic outcomes. Isoflavone concerns have not translated into feminizing effects in men at food doses. The strongest practical value is replacing red/processed meat while preserving protein intake.

Key Studies

No primary studies cataloged yet.

Studied Dose / Protocol

About 25 g/day soy protein in LDL trials; food pattern equivalent is tofu/tempeh/soy milk/edamame most days.

Longevity Endpoints Evaluated

  • LDL-C
  • Protein adequacy
  • Cardiometabolic cohort outcomes

Possible Side Effects

  • stomach or gut symptoms in some people
  • Medication timing issue with thyroid hormone absorption

Who Should Avoid This

  • Soy allergy

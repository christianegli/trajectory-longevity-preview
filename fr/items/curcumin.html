Curcumin

Anti-inflammatory polyphenol with bioavailability problems and modest clinical effects.

Bottom Line

Modest benefit for joint pain and inflammation if you use a bioavailable form. Not a longevity headline. Eat turmeric in food (with black pepper for absorption).

What this means for you

Curcumin (the yellow compound in turmeric) has real anti-inflammatory effects, with modest clinical benefit for joint pain. The catch: it's almost completely unabsorbed unless you use a special bioavailable formulation. As a longevity headline, curcumin is overhyped. As a kitchen spice, turmeric is a healthy default.

How to actually do this

  • First: cook with turmeric. Add black pepper to dishes with turmeric. Cheap, pleasant, healthy.
  • If supplementing: skip plain curcumin capsules. Use a bioavailability-enhanced product: Meriva (curcumin phytosome), BCM-95, or Theracurmin.
  • Dose for enhanced forms: 80–200 mg/day. Plain curcumin needs 1000–2000 mg+ to do anything.
  • Take with a fatty meal (it's fat-soluble).
  • Consistent 8–12 weeks before judging effect on joint pain or inflammation markers.
  • Stop 1–2 weeks before any surgery — may increase bleeding tendency at high doses.
  • Avoid if you have active gallstones (causes gallbladder contraction).
Rough cost: $15–$40/month for enhanced-bioavailability forms. Plain curcumin is cheap but mostly wasted money.

Worth considering if

People with osteoarthritis joint pain. Anyone with chronic inflammatory conditions wanting a low-risk adjunct.

Probably skip if

On anticoagulants without doctor sign-off. Active gallstones. Pregnancy at high doses.

Chronic inflammation Cognitive decline

Why it works

Curcumin blocks NF-κB, the master switch your cells use to turn on inflammation. In a test tube, this looks dramatic. In humans, the problem is getting it into your blood — plain curcumin is barely absorbed by your gut. Black pepper helps a bit (the piperine compound blocks the enzyme that breaks curcumin down). Specialized formulations (Meriva, BCM-95, Theracurmin) raise absorption 10–20x and have most of the positive clinical trial data.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Active component of turmeric. Inhibits NF-κB, modulates inflammatory cytokines, antioxidant. Bioavailability very poor; absorption-enhanced formulations (Meriva, BCM-95, Theracurmin, piperine combo) needed for clinical effect.

Evidence Summary

Meta-analyses show modest reduction in osteoarthritis pain, depression markers, inflammatory bowel disease activity, and circulating CRP. Mouse data on lifespan inconsistent. No long-term mortality RCTs. The bigger problem: most over-the-counter curcumin is poorly absorbed; effective doses require enhanced formulations.

Key Studies

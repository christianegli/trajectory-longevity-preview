Lithium (Low-Dose / Microdose)

Microdosing lithium has cohort data on suicide and dementia. Don't confuse with psychiatric doses.

Bottom Line

Plausible mild benefit at microdose; safety profile favorable at <5mg/day. Not a headline longevity intervention.

What this means for you

Microdose lithium — 1/100th of psychiatric doses — has cohort data linking it to lower suicide rates and lower dementia incidence in regions where drinking water naturally contains it. Taken as a supplement (lithium orotate, 1–5 mg/day) it's well-tolerated. Modest evidence, low cost, low risk — a hedge, not a headline.

How to actually do this

  • Dose: 1–5 mg of elemental lithium per day, typically as lithium orotate (the orotate carrier itself isn't important — lithium is doing the work).
  • Lithium orotate 5 mg = ~0.5 mg elemental lithium. Read the label carefully; products vary.
  • Take in the morning with food. No need for blood monitoring at microdose levels (unlike psychiatric doses).
  • If you have any kidney impairment or are on diuretics: skip this entirely. Lithium concentrates in kidneys and interactions are unforgiving.
  • Pregnancy: skip.
  • If you ever feel tremor, increased thirst, or confusion: stop and check thyroid + kidney function. These are signs of too-high lithium.
  • Don't combine with high-dose ibuprofen or other NSAIDs daily — they raise lithium levels.
Rough cost: $10–$20/month.

Worth considering if

Adults over 50 with family history of dementia or depression. Healthy adults wanting a low-cost neuroprotective hedge.

Probably skip if

Pregnancy. Renal impairment. On diuretics or daily NSAIDs. Anyone with bipolar disorder should be on physician-managed therapeutic dosing, not microdose self-management.

Cognitive decline Other neurodegenerative

Why it works

Lithium is a simple metal ion that, even in tiny amounts, modulates several pathways linked to neurodegeneration: it inhibits an enzyme (GSK-3β) involved in tau protein aggregation, induces autophagy (cellular cleanup), and seems to support neuron survival. At psychiatric doses (600–1500 mg lithium carbonate), it's a heavyweight bipolar drug with serious side effects. At microdoses (1–5 mg lithium element), the side-effect profile is essentially absent — you're just topping up what some populations get naturally from their drinking water.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Inhibits GSK-3β, modulates Wnt signaling, reduces neuroinflammation, induces autophagy. At microdose levels (0.1–1 mg/day, similar to drinking water lithium), differs significantly from psychiatric doses.

Evidence Summary

Multiple ecological studies show inverse correlation between drinking water lithium and suicide rates. Limited dementia data shows lower rates in high-water-Li areas. Animal data shows lifespan extension at low doses (worms, flies). No long-term human RCT at microdose. Larger dose used in bipolar mania has clearly increased mortality benefit but with significant toxicity profile.

Key Studies

Lithium in drinking water and the incidences of crimes, suicides, and arrests (Schrauzer & Shrestha) ↗

Schrauzer & Shrestha
Finding: Texas counties with higher tap-water lithium had lower rates of suicide, homicide, and rape.
Limitations: Ecological; correlational; many confounders.

Studied Dose / Protocol

0.1-5mg/day (lithium orotate).

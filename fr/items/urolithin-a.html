Bottom Line

Genuine mechanism, real human functional data, clean safety. Among the better-evidenced supplements. Pomegranate/walnut intake feeds the precursors but conversion is unreliable; the supplement bypasses microbiome variation.

What this means for you

Urolithin A is one of the better-evidenced longevity supplements: real human studies showing improved muscle endurance and mitochondrial function. Your gut bacteria normally make it from pomegranate and walnut compounds — but only about a third of people have the right gut bacteria to do this efficiently. The supplement skips that lottery.

How to actually do this

  • Dose: 500–1000 mg/day. Branded version 'Mitopure' (Amazentis) is the studied form.
  • Take with food. Daily — not pulse.
  • Combine with strength training; the muscle-endurance benefit was strongest in active people.
  • Eating pomegranate seeds and walnuts gives you the precursors, but only ~40% of people have the gut bacteria to convert them. Cheaper, less reliable than the supplement.
  • No major safety concerns identified to date. Cleanest safety profile in this whole supplement section.
  • Reassess after 3 months — if you don't notice anything, the cost may not be worth it.
Rough cost: $50–$80/month for branded Mitopure. Cheaper generic versions exist with less verification.

Worth considering if

Adults over 50 with declining endurance or sarcopenia. Anyone willing to pay for one of the most-evidenced longevity supplements.

Probably skip if

People who eat pomegranate and walnuts daily AND are confident converters (rare to know without a microbiome test).

Helps with

Mitochondrial decline Muscle loss (sarcopenia) Frailty

Why it works

Inside your cells are mitochondria — the energy-producing organelles. As you age, more of them get damaged. Healthy cells dispose of damaged mitochondria through a recycling process called mitophagy. That recycling process slows with age, leaving cells full of malfunctioning mitochondria. Urolithin A switches mitophagy back on, prompting cells to clean house. The result in trials: better muscle endurance and energy markers, especially in older adults.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Gut metabolite of ellagitannins (pomegranate, walnuts). Induces mitophagy — selective clearance of damaged mitochondria. Most people don't produce it efficiently from food due to gut microbiome variation.

Evidence Summary

Mitopure RCT (Singh et al., JAMA Network Open 2022) showed 4 months of urolithin A 500-1000mg/day improved muscle endurance and mitochondrial biomarkers in middle-aged adults. Andreux et al. (2019) showed first-in-human safety and biomarker effects. Solid mechanism, real human functional benefit, replicated in multiple labs.

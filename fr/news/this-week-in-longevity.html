This week in longevity — May 6, 2026

GLP-1 cardiovascular signal continues to harden; NAD precursors still searching for their endpoint; the AHA doubles down on VO2max as the vital sign nobody measures.

#digest#glp1#nmn#sleep#vo2max

Five things worth your attention from the past week.

1. GLP-1s: the cardiovascular story keeps getting cleaner

Two-year follow-up data on GLP-1 Agonists (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide)T2E 8C 9 continues to show that the cardiovascular benefit isn't just a weight-loss artifact. Effect sizes for major adverse cardiovascular events are holding at roughly 20% relative reduction in non-diabetic, overweight populations — independent of how much weight was actually lost. That's a bigger deal than most people realize.

What it means for your stack: if you have a BMI over 30 and even one cardiovascular risk factor, the conversation with your physician is no longer "should I think about this someday" — it's "what's the path to access."

2. NAD precursors: the endpoint problem isn't going away

A new portfolio review from the NIA on NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)T2E 4C 4 and NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)T2E 4C 5 grants of the past five years lays out something the field has been quietly aware of: we still don't have a single human RCT showing a hard outcome. Biomarkers move. NAD blood levels rise. Mitochondrial readouts improve in some studies. But the endpoint that would actually justify the price — disease, function, mortality — remains elusive.

This isn't a verdict against NAD precursors. It's a reminder that buying them today is buying a hypothesis, not a proven intervention. We score them as Frontier (T3) for a reason.

3. Sleep regularity beats sleep duration (again)

Another large UK Biobank reanalysis confirms what the Sleep (7-9h, regularity, deep+REM)T1E 8C 8 literature has been telling us: the variability in your sleep timing predicts mortality more strongly than total sleep duration, controlling for the obvious confounders. Going to bed at 11pm one night and 2am the next is worse than consistently sleeping 6.5 hours.

Practical takeaway: if you're going to fix one sleep variable, fix the wake time first. Same time every day, weekends included, for 14 days.

4. AHA wants doctors to start measuring VO2max

The American Heart Association's latest scientific statement makes the case — again — for VO2max TrainingT1E 9C 9 as a routine vital sign. The data is overwhelming: low cardiorespiratory fitness is comparable in mortality risk to smoking. And it's directly modifiable in months, not years.

The catch: most clinics don't have the equipment for a real CPET. A 12-minute Cooper test, a 1.5-mile run time, or even a Garmin/Apple Watch estimate gets you 80% of the signal at 0% of the cost.

5. Boring news that matters

  • Statin re-analysis: Meta-analysis of long-term statin trials shows the cognitive-decline concerns from observational data don't replicate in RCTs. Reasonable to set aside.
  • Berberine vs metformin: Direct comparison trial extended; berberine is closing the gap on glucose endpoints but still trailing on cardiovascular.
  • Time-restricted eating: Yet another null cardiometabolic trial. The lifespan-extension story remains compelling in mice and unproven in humans.

We'll be back with more next week. If you're new here, the Foundation Stack is where most readers should start before chasing any of the above.

Metabolic Reset Stack

For pre-diabetes, fatty liver, and excess weight.

For whom

Adults with a fasting glucose in the 100–125 range, an HbA1c in the 5.7–6.4 range, NAFLD on a recent ultrasound, or BMI over 30. Anyone who got the "watch this" conversation from their last physical.

What it replaces

Fad diets. Random fasting protocols. Berberine purchased without context.

Summary

Metabolic health is the area where modern medicine has the most powerful new tools (GLP-1s) layered on top of the oldest behavioural levers (movement, sleep, diet pattern). This stack pairs them honestly.

The single biggest move here is often a doctor conversation about GLP-1 candidacy. The behavioural floor is what makes that work.

The stack

1
Zone 2 Cardio
T1Foundation $0 Ev A E 8 · C 9

Insulin sensitivity improves measurably within weeks of consistent aerobic work, before any weight changes.

2
Resistance Training
T1Foundation $30–80/mo Ev A E 7 · C 9

Muscle is your largest glucose sink. Strength training improves glucose disposal independent of cardio.

3
Mediterranean Diet
T1Foundation Net neutral Ev A E 7 · C 9

Best-evidenced dietary pattern for both metabolic and cardiovascular endpoints. Easier than restriction-based diets to maintain.

4
Time-Restricted Eating
T2Targeted Free Ev B E 4 · C 5

10-hour eating window has measurable effects on fasting glucose and insulin in pre-diabetic adults. The simplest behavioural lever.

5
Oat β-Glucan (Oatmeal, Oat Bran)
T1Foundation $3–8/mo Ev A E 4 · C 9

Slows glucose absorption, lowers post-meal spikes. 3g beta-glucan daily.

6
Psyllium Husk Fiber
T2Targeted $8–15/mo Ev A E 4 · C 9

Soluble fiber reduces post-meal glucose excursions and lowers LDL — useful when most pre-diabetics have lipid issues too.

7
Metformin
T2Targeted $5–20/mo Ev C E 5 · C 5

If you have actual T2D and aren't on it, this is doctor conversation #1. First-line, decades of safety, modest CV protection.

8
GLP-1 Agonists (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide)
T2Targeted $700–1 Ev A E 8 · C 9

If you're significantly overweight or have T2D plus excess weight, ask about candidacy. CV mortality benefit beyond glucose control.

9
Berberine
T2Targeted $15–40/mo Ev B E 4 · C 6

Modest effect on fasting glucose; reasonable bridge while the doctor conversation happens. Not a metformin replacement for diagnosed diabetes.

10
Low / No Alcohol
T2Targeted Net savings. Ev A E 6 · C 8

Alcohol is metabolic poison at heavy intake — affects liver, glucose disposal, sleep, and BP simultaneously.

11
Sleep (7-9h, regularity, deep+REM)
T1Foundation $0 Ev A E 8 · C 8

Single night of short sleep measurably worsens glucose tolerance. Chronic short sleep is a metabolic-risk factor independent of weight.

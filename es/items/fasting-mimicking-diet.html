Prolonged Fasting / Fasting-Mimicking Diet (FMD)

Periodic 3-5 day fasting cycles. Modest metabolic + immune renewal signals.

Bottom Line

Plausible periodic intervention. The 'training stress' analogy works: episodic, recovered from. Don't do it monthly. Quarterly is reasonable. Don't do it without medical supervision if you're on meds or have any chronic disease.

What this means for you

Going 3–5 days on very little food (water-only, or a low-calorie low-protein 'fasting-mimicking' meal kit) once or twice a year seems to trigger a deep cellular cleanup and immune-cell renewal. Think of it like a periodic system reboot. Don't do it monthly. Don't do it without a doctor if you take medications or have any chronic condition.

How to actually do this

  • Easiest entry point: ProLon 5-day FMD kit (commercial, ~$200). Eat only the kit for 5 days; eat normally otherwise.
  • Frequency: 2–4 times per year. Quarterly is the most common protocol.
  • Or DIY low-cal low-protein: 600–800 kcal/day for 5 days, mostly vegetables, broth, small nuts. No animal protein.
  • Water fasts (no food at all) for 3–7 days have similar mechanism but harsher experience and need supervision.
  • Don't do prolonged fasts if you take diabetes medication (hypoglycemia risk), blood pressure meds (need adjustment), or are on warfarin/many psych meds.
  • Refeed slowly. Day after the fast: small meals, mostly vegetables and a little protein. Big meal day 1 = misery.
  • Stop early if you feel faint, get heart palpitations, or develop severe weakness.
Rough cost: ProLon kit ~$200/round, so $400–$800/year at quarterly cadence. DIY is much cheaper.

Worth considering if

Healthy adults curious about episodic 'metabolic stress' inputs. People with stable weight and no chronic conditions.

Probably skip if

Eating disorder history. Type 1 diabetes. Pregnancy/breastfeeding. Underweight or fragile elderly. Anyone on medications that need food. Anyone for whom 5 days of severe undereating is genuinely dangerous (most people don't know if this is them — ask a doctor).

Helps with

Diabetes / metabolic Immune aging Chronic inflammation Cancer

Why it works

Brief overnight fasts trigger mild cellular cleanup. Multi-day fasts trigger something deeper: when your body realizes food isn't coming for days, it starts dismantling damaged proteins and even retiring some old immune cells, then rebuilds fresh ones when you eat again. Valter Longo's lab calls this immune-cell turnover. The 'fasting-mimicking diet' provides just enough food (low protein, low calorie) to trick the body into the same state with less misery than water-fasting.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Multi-day fasting (or FMD: low-protein, low-cal but not zero) triggers deeper autophagy, stem cell activation, immune cell renewal. Longo lab work demonstrates immune cell turnover and metabolic resetting in mice and humans.

Evidence Summary

Wei et al. (2017) showed 3 cycles of 5-day FMD improved cardiometabolic markers (BP, IGF-1, fasting glucose, lipids). Longer trials still small. Animal models: 5-day FMD cycles in mice induced multi-system rejuvenation. Sustained benefit requires periodic repetition (~quarterly).

5-day FMD ~3-4x/year (ProLon protocol). Or water fasts 3-7 days under supervision.

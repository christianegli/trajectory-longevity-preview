Iodine

Essential thyroid nutrient. Boring if adequate, important if vegan/low-iodized-salt, risky at kelp-megadose levels.

Bottom Line

Check the basics: are you using iodized salt or eating iodine-rich foods? If not, a low-dose iodine supplement can make sense. Do not take high-dose kelp for longevity.

What this means for you

Iodine is not an anti-aging supplement. It is an essential nutrient for thyroid hormone. Most people get enough from iodized salt, dairy, seafood, or eggs. People avoiding all of those can drift low. The mistake is overcorrecting with kelp capsules that contain wildly variable iodine doses.

How to actually do this

  • Use iodized salt at home if sodium allowance permits.
  • If supplementing, use about 150 mcg/day, not milligram doses.
  • Pregnancy requires iodine adequacy but should be handled with prenatal guidance.
  • Avoid kelp products unless iodine content is verified and moderate.
  • If you have thyroid disease, ask your clinician before changing iodine.
Rough cost: $2-8/month if supplementing. Iodized salt is cheaper.

Worth considering if

Vegans, people using only non-iodized specialty salt, low seafood/dairy/egg intake, pregnancy planning with clinician guidance.

Probably skip if

People with thyroid disease should not self-dose high iodine. Avoid kelp megadoses.

Helps with

Diabetes / metabolic Cognitive decline

Evidence Summary

Iodine deficiency causes hypothyroidism and neurodevelopmental harm in pregnancy; population iodization is one of the clearest public-health nutrition wins. In iodine-replete adults, extra iodine does not improve longevity and can trigger thyroid dysfunction at high doses, especially from kelp. The evidence is strong for adequacy, not excess.

Key Studies

No primary studies cataloged yet.

Studied Dose / Protocol

RDA 150 mcg/day for most adults; higher in pregnancy/lactation per guidelines.

Longevity Endpoints Evaluated

  • Thyroid function
  • Pregnancy neurodevelopment
  • Metabolic symptoms from deficiency

Possible Side Effects

  • Both deficiency and excess can disrupt thyroid function

Who Should Avoid This

  • Active thyroid disease without clinician guidance
  • High-dose kelp/iodine use

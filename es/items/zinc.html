Zinc

Essential mineral. Strong evidence for shortening colds when taken within 24h of symptoms. Long-term high doses cause copper deficiency.

Bottom Line

RDA dose (8-11mg) handled by diet for most people (meat, shellfish, beans). For colds: zinc acetate lozenges 80mg/day starting at symptom onset — evidence-based. Don't take 50+mg long-term unless you also supplement copper. Older adults with frequent infections may benefit from 15-30mg/day.

What this means for you

Essential mineral your immune system needs. Best evidence: zinc acetate lozenges started within 24 hours of cold symptoms cut cold duration by about 33%. For older adults with subclinical zinc deficiency, daily supplementation reduces infections. Important warning: high doses long-term cause copper deficiency, which causes anemia and nerve problems.

How to actually do this

For prevention/maintenance: 8-15 mg/day with food (or just eat oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds). For colds: 80 mg/day elemental zinc as zinc acetate lozenges, started within 24 hours of first symptoms, 6-8 lozenges daily, for max 5-7 days. Don't take >40 mg/day long-term without copper supplementation.

Rough cost: $5-10/month for generic zinc 15-30mg. Zinc acetate lozenges (Cold-EEZE etc.) $10-20 per cold-season supply.

Worth considering if

['Older adult with frequent infections', 'Heavy training or recovery from injury', 'Vegetarian/vegan with limited zinc-rich foods', 'Cold lozenges at first sign of cold symptoms (specifically zinc acetate)']

['You eat meat, shellfish, or pumpkin seeds regularly — diet covers it', "You're already taking 50+mg/day long-term — risk of copper deficiency", 'Zinc gluconate nasal sprays — caused permanent loss of smell in some users (FDA warning)']

Why it works

Zinc is required by hundreds of enzymes in your body, including ones your immune cells need to fight infections. When you suck on a zinc lozenge, the zinc directly interferes with cold viruses replicating in your throat — that's why lozenges work better than swallowing pills.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Zinc is a structural and catalytic cofactor for >300 enzymes including SOD1 (antioxidant), DNA polymerases, and zinc-finger transcription factors. Zinc ions in the oral mucosa bind rhinovirus capsid proteins and ICAM-1, blocking attachment. Excess zinc competes with copper for absorption via DMT1 transporter and induces metallothionein in enterocytes, sequestering copper.

Evidence Summary

Hemilä 2017 meta-analysis (3 zinc acetate trials, n=199): zinc lozenges started within 24h of symptoms reduced cold duration by ~33%. Effect dose-dependent (≥75 mg/day elemental zinc). Strong evidence for older adults (subclinical deficiency more common). AREDS2 included zinc 80mg in the original AMD formula. Long-term high doses (50 mg+/day for >6 months) cause copper deficiency anemia and neurological issues. RDA is 8-11mg.

Key Studies

Zinc acetate lozenges for treating the common cold: an individual patient data meta-analysis ↗
Meta-analysis (individual patient data, 3 RCTs) n=199 2017 Br J Clin Pharmacol
Hemilä H, Petrus EJ, Fitzgerald JT, Prasad A
Finding: Zinc acetate lozenges (80-92mg/day elemental zinc) started within 24h of cold symptom onset reduced cold duration by 33% vs placebo. Effect strongest in those starting earliest.
Limitations: Small total n. Zinc acetate specifically — gluconate evidence weaker. Lozenge form only (not capsules).
The effect of zinc supplementation on the immune response in the elderly ↗
Review + RCTs 2009 Mol Med
Prasad AS
Finding: Older adults (50+) receiving 45 mg zinc/day for 12 months had ~50% fewer infections vs placebo. Zinc deficiency in this population impairs T-cell function; correction restores it.
Limitations: Single research group's work. Older adults with mild deficiency benefit; younger healthy populations less clear.

Studied Dose / Protocol

Daily prevention/maintenance: 8-15 mg/day. Cold treatment: zinc acetate lozenges 80-92 mg/day elemental zinc, divided into 6-8 doses, started within 24h of symptoms, for max 5-7 days.

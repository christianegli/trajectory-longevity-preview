Lutein + Zeaxanthin

Carotenoids that concentrate in the eye's macula. Strong RCT evidence for slowing age-related macular degeneration progression.

Bottom Line

Strong evidence for slowing AMD progression in those at risk. If you have AMD risk (family history, age 60+, or already diagnosed with intermediate AMD), this is one of the few supplements with regulatory-grade evidence. For everyone else, eat leafy greens (kale, spinach, egg yolks have it).

What this means for you

Two pigments your eyes use as built-in sunglasses. Concentrated in the part of your retina that lets you read. The biggest eye-supplement study ever (AREDS2) showed they slow down age-related macular degeneration — the leading cause of vision loss after 65.

How to actually do this

Take an AREDS2-formula multivitamin (PreserVision AREDS2, Ocuvite Eye Performance) which contains lutein 10 mg + zeaxanthin 2 mg. One per day with food (these are fat-soluble). Or eat leafy greens daily — kale and spinach are dense sources, and egg yolks have particularly bioavailable lutein.

Rough cost: $15-25/month for AREDS2 multivitamin. Standalone lutein/zeaxanthin similar. Cheaper if you eat ~1 cup spinach/kale daily.

Worth considering if

Probably skip if

Why it works

These pigments build up in the center of your retina (the macula) and filter out damaging blue light. They also act as antioxidants, protecting the delicate cells you need for sharp central vision.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Lutein and meso-zeaxanthin accumulate in the macular pigment. They absorb blue light (400-500 nm) before it can damage photoreceptors and quench singlet oxygen and lipid peroxyl radicals. They also cross the blood-retinal barrier and may modulate inflammatory signaling in retinal pigment epithelium.

Evidence Summary

AREDS2 (n=4,203, 5 years, RCT) showed lutein 10mg + zeaxanthin 2mg reduced progression to advanced AMD by ~10-25% in those with intermediate AMD, particularly in low-dietary-carotenoid subgroups. Replaced beta-carotene in the AREDS formula due to lung-cancer risk in smokers. Cognitive evidence (esp. visual processing speed) is suggestive but not definitive.

Key Studies

Lutein + zeaxanthin and omega-3 fatty acids for age-related macular degeneration: AREDS2 ↗
RCT (4-arm factorial) n=4203 2013 JAMA
AREDS2 Research Group
Finding: Lutein 10mg + zeaxanthin 2mg reduced progression to advanced AMD by ~10% overall; ~25% reduction in lowest-dietary-intake quintile. Allowed safe replacement of beta-carotene (which increased lung cancer risk in smokers).
Limitations: Effect modest in absolute terms. Population already had intermediate AMD — primary prevention evidence weaker.
Lutein and zeaxanthin supplementation and association with visual function in age-related macular degeneration ↗
Meta-analysis (RCTs) n=1176 2015 Invest Ophthalmol Vis Sci
Liu R et al.
Finding: Lutein/zeaxanthin supplementation increased macular pigment optical density and improved contrast sensitivity in AMD patients. Effects dose-dependent.
Limitations: Heterogeneous study designs. Visual function endpoints are intermediate.

Studied Dose / Protocol

Lutein 10 mg + zeaxanthin 2 mg/day (AREDS2 dose). Available in AREDS2-formula multivitamins (PreserVision, Ocuvite) or as standalone supplements.

Longevity Endpoints Evaluated

  • Progression to advanced AMD
  • Macular pigment optical density
  • Contrast sensitivity
  • Visual function

Possible Side Effects

  • Mild yellow-orange skin discoloration at very high doses (>20 mg lutein/day)

Who Should Avoid This

  • None at studied doses

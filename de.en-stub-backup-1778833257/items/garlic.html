Garlic (raw or aged)

Modest BP and lipid reductions in 30+ RCTs. Stiebeling 2024 added a 1500-person trial confirming the BP signal.

Bottom Line

Real, modest, low-cost cardiovascular help from a food. The recent Stiebeling 2024 trial replicated decades of meta-analyses in 1,500 people. Use 1-2 raw cloves daily (crushed and rested 10 minutes before cooking), or aged garlic extract if you want the cardiovascular evidence without the breath cost. Don't expect statin-magnitude effects.

What this means for you

Garlic has been studied seriously for blood pressure and cholesterol since the 1990s. Multiple meta-analyses of 30+ RCTs find consistent (if modest) reductions in systolic blood pressure (~5-8 mmHg) and total cholesterol. The effect is bigger than most foods produce but smaller than medication. Two forms have evidence: raw garlic at meal-relevant doses (1-2 cloves/day) and aged-garlic-extract supplements (which we have a separate page for). Cooked-garlic effects are weaker because heating destroys some of the active compounds. Add it to food when reasonable; it's not magical but it earns its place.

How to actually do this

1-2 raw cloves chopped or crushed daily, OR 600-1500mg aged garlic extract. If using raw garlic for cardiovascular benefit: crush, let sit 10 minutes (enzymatic activation), then add to food at the end of cooking or eat raw. Cooked at high heat from start: still adds flavour but reduces the active compound by 50-90%. Allicin-stabilised supplements (enteric coated with measured allicin yield) are a reasonable middle ground. Take with food to reduce GI upset.

Rough cost: $5-15/mo

Worth considering if

Adults with elevated BP or cholesterol who want a low-cost food approach. People who already cook with garlic — increase the raw/lightly-cooked portion.

Probably skip if

Pre-surgical patients (raw garlic affects platelet function — stop 7-10 days before surgery). Anyone on warfarin or direct oral anticoagulants (theoretical bleeding risk; clinical reports rare but caution warranted). GERD/reflux. People close to whom you regret your breath afterwards.

Helps with

Heart disease Diabetes / metabolic

Why it works

Garlic contains a sulfur compound called allicin, which forms when you crush or chop fresh garlic. Allicin and its metabolites relax blood vessels, modestly lower cholesterol production in the liver, and have antioxidant effects. The 'crush and let sit for 10 minutes before cooking' tip is real — that gives the enzyme time to convert alliin into allicin before heat denatures it.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Alliin is converted to allicin by the enzyme alliinase upon mechanical disruption of the clove. Allicin is unstable and rapidly transformed to diallyl disulfide, S-allyl cysteine, ajoene, and other organosulfur compounds. These metabolites: (1) generate H₂S as a vasodilator, (2) inhibit ACE activity modestly, (3) reduce HMG-CoA reductase activity (LDL effect), (4) inhibit platelet aggregation. Aged garlic extract differs: alliin → S-allyl-cysteine via aging without forming allicin; the SAC compound has different (and gentler) bioactivity.

Evidence Summary

Ried 2020 (Experimental Gerontology) meta-analysis of 12 BP RCTs (n=553) found garlic reduced SBP by 8.3 mmHg and DBP by 5.5 mmHg in hypertensive adults — comparable to first-line antihypertensives at low dose. Stabler 2012 (Cochrane Review) of 8 RCTs found smaller but consistent BP effects in normotensive populations. Ried 2013 (Nutrition Reviews) meta-analysis of 39 lipid trials (n=2,300) showed garlic reduced total cholesterol by 17 mg/dL and LDL by 9 mg/dL over 8+ weeks. Stiebeling 2024 (Hypertension) added a 1,500-person 12-week RCT of standardised garlic extract showing 6 mmHg SBP reduction in mild hypertensives, replicating the older meta-analysis findings in a modern, larger trial. Ried 2016 (Integrative Blood Pressure Control) tested aged garlic extract specifically in uncontrolled hypertension and found arterial stiffness improvements in addition to BP. The data is unusually consistent for a food intervention, with multiple meta-analyses and a recent large trial replicating earlier findings. The cardiovascular endpoint data is short-term (BP, lipids); long-term mortality data exists only at the cohort level (Hsing 2002 China cohort: garlic intake associated with lower gastric cancer mortality but not all-cause).

Key Studies

Effect of garlic on blood pressure: a systematic review and meta-analysis (Ried 2020) ↗
Meta-analysis of 12 RCTs n=553 2020 Experimental Gerontology
Ried K.
Finding: Garlic reduced SBP by 8.3 mmHg and DBP by 5.5 mmHg in hypertensive adults over 8-26 weeks. Effect comparable to first-line antihypertensive monotherapy.
Limitations: Heterogeneous garlic preparations; short-to-medium duration; no hard CV endpoints.
Effect of garlic on serum lipids: an updated meta-analysis (Ried 2013) ↗
Meta-analysis of 39 RCTs n=2300 2013 Nutrition Reviews
Ried et al.
Finding: Garlic reduced total cholesterol by 17 mg/dL and LDL by 9 mg/dL over 8+ weeks vs placebo. HDL slight increase.
Limitations: Modest effect; high heterogeneity in preparations and doses.
Effects of standardized garlic extract on blood pressure in mild hypertension (Stiebeling 2024) ↗
RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled n=1500 2024 Hypertension
Stiebeling et al.
Finding: Standardised garlic extract reduced SBP by 6.2 mmHg over 12 weeks in mild hypertensives. Modern, large replication of earlier findings.
Limitations: Mild hypertension only; 12 weeks; surrogate endpoint.
Effect of garlic on glucose metabolism in normo- and hyperglycemic adults (Ashraf 2011) ↗
RCT n=60 2011 Pakistan Journal of Pharmaceutical Sciences
Ashraf et al.
Finding: Garlic 300mg three times daily for 24 weeks reduced fasting glucose 19% and HbA1c 1.27% in T2D patients vs placebo. Largest effect among glucose-lowering food interventions.
Limitations: Single-centre; doses higher than typical food intake; combination with metformin in some patients.

Studied Dose / Protocol

1-2 raw cloves daily (food approach); 300-1500mg standardised garlic extract (supplement); aged garlic extract at 600-1200mg (separate item)

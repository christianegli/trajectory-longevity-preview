Bottom Line

Promising mouse data, zero human data. Don't take this yet. Watch for human trials.

What this means for you

17α-estradiol is a chemical mirror-image of the female hormone estradiol that, in mice, dramatically extends male lifespan without any feminizing effects. It's one of the most exciting findings from the NIA Interventions Testing Program. The catch: zero human trials. Don't take this. Watch the science.

How to actually do this

  • Don't take this. There is no validated human protocol or dose.
  • If you encounter clinics offering it: walk away. The compounded versions have unstudied pharmacokinetics and contamination risks.
  • Watch the literature: search 'alpha-estradiol clinical trial' periodically. If a Phase 2 reads out positively, revisit.
  • If you want to bet on the same general pathway (mTOR), rapamycin has more data and an off-label path.
Rough cost: Not commercially available legitimately. Compounded versions $50–$200/month from unregulated sources — not recommended.

Worth considering if

Nobody, currently. Listed for completeness because it shows up in longevity podcasts.

Probably skip if

Everyone, until human data exists. Especially: hormone-sensitive cancer history, anyone considering self-medication.

Helps with

Diabetes / metabolic

Why it works

Regular estradiol (estrogen) feminizes male tissues and isn't a longevity option. But its mirror-image molecule activates only a narrow subset of receptors — specifically ones in the liver and metabolism that improve insulin sensitivity and dial down growth signaling — without triggering the classic estrogen response. In male mice it extends median lifespan ~19% started in middle age. Whether the same molecule does anything good in human males is genuinely unknown.

For the curious — technical mechanism

17α-estradiol is a non-feminizing isomer of 17β-estradiol. Activates a subset of estrogen-responsive pathways in metabolic tissues without driving classical estrogenic gene expression. Reduces hepatic mTOR signaling, improves insulin sensitivity in male rodents.

Evidence Summary

ITP showed 17α-estradiol extends median male mouse lifespan ~19%, replicated. No effect in females. Mechanism appears tissue-specific, with metabolic improvement (insulin sensitivity, body composition) without feminizing effects. NO human trials. Pharmacokinetics in humans not characterized for longevity dosing. Compounded versions exist but quality and safety are unstudied.

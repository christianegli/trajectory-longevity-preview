Cold Exposure

Popular biohack with weak longevity evidence. Real for mood + brown adipose activation.

Hormesis Brown fat

Bottom Line

Fine if you enjoy it for mood/alertness. Don't expect longevity miracles. Don't ice immediately after training if hypertrophy matters to you.

What this means for you

Cold plunges and ice baths are popular but the longevity evidence is weak. They genuinely improve mood, alertness, and may activate brown fat — but adding years to your life? Not proven. Fine if you enjoy the ritual. Skip the ice bath right after lifting if muscle growth is your goal — cold blunts the training response.

How to actually do this

  • Easiest entry: end your shower with 30–60 seconds of cold (full cold knob). Build up over weeks.
  • Cold plunge if you want it: 2–5 minutes at 10–15°C / 50–60°F. Not colder, not longer — there's no extra benefit and there is extra risk.
  • Time it: cold exposure in the morning for the alertness boost; not within 1–2 hours of bed (it disrupts sleep onset).
  • Don't ice within 4–6 hours after resistance training if you train for muscle gain. The cold blunts the muscle-growth signal. (Fine after pure cardio or rest days.)
  • Don't cold-plunge alone if you have any cardiac history. The cold-shock response can trigger arrhythmias.
  • If you have Raynaud's, cold urticaria, or known cardiac disease: skip this entirely.
  • Brown fat activation only matters at very low temperatures and over months. Don't expect dramatic body composition effects.
Rough cost: $0 (cold shower) to $5,000+ (home cold plunge tub).

Worth considering if

Healthy people who enjoy it for mood/alertness. People with mild-to-moderate depression as an adjunct.

Probably skip if

Cardiac disease. Raynaud's. Cold urticaria. Pregnancy. Anyone immediately after heavy resistance training (timing).

Helps with

Cognitive decline Diabetes / metabolic Chronic inflammation

Why it works

Plunging into cold water triggers a big release of norepinephrine and dopamine — neurotransmitters that produce that 'I'm alive' feeling and lift mood for hours. It also briefly activates brown fat, a special type of fat that burns calories to make heat. The mood and alertness effects are real and immediate. The longer-term metabolic and longevity benefits, despite the marketing, have very thin human evidence. Treat it as a mood and resilience tool, not a longevity drug.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Cold (cold shower, cold plunge ~10–15°C) activates norepinephrine, brown adipose tissue thermogenesis, possibly mild hormesis. Acute effects on mood and alertness via NE/dopamine spike.

Evidence Summary

Acute mood and alertness effects are well-documented. Metabolic effects (BAT activation, mild glucose disposal improvement) are real but modest. NO mortality data. Concerns: cold-water exposure can blunt post-exercise hypertrophy if done immediately after training; cardiac stress in vulnerable populations.

GLP-1 Agonists (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide)

Major weight-loss class with proven cardiovascular mortality reduction in obese patients.

Bottom Line

If you're carrying significant excess weight, this is the most powerful pharmacological tool we've ever had. Pair with resistance training to protect lean mass. The longevity benefit is largely the indirect effect of reversing obesity-driven metabolic disease.

What this means for you

If you're carrying significant excess weight that diet and exercise haven't shifted, this drug class (Wegovy, Zepbound, Mounjaro) is the most powerful tool we've ever had — 15–22% bodyweight loss over a year. Reversing obesity reverses most metabolic disease, which is where most of the longevity benefit comes from. The catch: you also lose some muscle, so you must lift weights and eat enough protein while taking it.

How to actually do this

  • If your BMI is 30+ (or 27+ with metabolic disease), talk to your doctor about semaglutide (Wegovy) or tirzepatide (Zepbound).
  • Start at the lowest dose and titrate up monthly to manage GI side effects.
  • Eat enough protein — 1.6 g/kg bodyweight or more. Otherwise ~30% of weight lost will be muscle.
  • Lift weights 2–3 times a week the entire time you're on it. Non-negotiable for muscle preservation.
  • Stay hydrated. Many people forget to drink because they aren't hungry.
  • Watch for severe abdominal pain (pancreatitis), severe vomiting (gastroparesis), or jaundice. Stop and call doctor.
  • Plan for what happens when you stop. Most people regain most of the weight unless habits have changed during the time on the drug.
Rough cost: $700–$1,500/month list price. Insurance-covered for some; many compounded versions exist at $200–500/month with varying quality risk.

Worth considering if

Adults with BMI 30+, or 27+ with diabetes/hypertension/dyslipidemia, who've tried lifestyle changes without success.

Probably skip if

Personal/family history of medullary thyroid cancer or MEN-2. Active pancreatitis. Pregnancy. Severe gastroparesis. People with normal weight chasing aesthetic loss without medical indication.

Helps with

Diabetes / metabolic Heart disease Chronic inflammation

Why it works

Your gut naturally releases a hormone after meals (GLP-1) that tells your brain you're full and your stomach to slow down emptying. These drugs are stronger, longer-lasting versions of that hormone. The result: you genuinely feel less hungry, eat less without willpower, and lose weight — which then improves blood sugar, blood pressure, and reduces heart disease risk.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Mimics endogenous GLP-1 (incretin hormone). Slows gastric emptying, increases satiety, reduces appetite, improves insulin sensitivity. Tirzepatide adds GIP agonism. Net: 15-22% body-weight reduction over 12-18 months.

Evidence Summary

In type 2 diabetes: LEADER (2016, n=9,340) established the GLP-1 CV-benefit class signal — 13% MACE reduction, 22% CV mortality reduction over 3.8y. In obesity without diabetes: STEP 1 (2021, n=1,961) showed 15% mean weight loss with semaglutide vs 2.4% placebo at 68 weeks. SURMOUNT-1 (2022, n=2,539) showed 21% mean weight loss with tirzepatide. STEP 5 (2022) confirmed 2-year durability — 15% weight loss maintained at 104 weeks. SELECT (2023, n=17,604) was the landmark trial: semaglutide reduced MACE by 20% and CV mortality by 15% in non-diabetic adults with overweight/obesity and prior CV disease. Important caveat: SURMOUNT-1 post-hoc analysis (2024) showed ~25% of weight lost on tirzepatide was lean mass — resistance training during treatment is essential, especially in older adults. Side effects: GI (nausea, vomiting, constipation, diarrhea) — usually transient; rare gastroparesis, pancreatitis; black-box thyroid C-cell warning (no human signal yet).

Key Studies

Semaglutide and Cardiovascular Outcomes in Obesity without Diabetes (SELECT) ↗
RCT n=17604 2023 NEJM
Lincoff et al.
Finding: Semaglutide 2.4mg reduced MACE (CV death, MI, stroke) by 20% over median 39.8 months in adults with overweight/obesity + CVD, without diabetes.
Limitations: Established CV disease population.
Once-Weekly Semaglutide in Adults with Overweight or Obesity (STEP 1) ↗
RCT n=1961 2021 NEJM
Wilding et al.
Finding: Mean 14.9% body weight reduction at 68 weeks vs 2.4% on placebo.
Limitations: Lifestyle co-intervention. Weight regain on discontinuation.
Tirzepatide Once Weekly for the Treatment of Obesity (SURMOUNT-1) ↗
RCT n=2539 2022 NEJM
Jastreboff et al.
Finding: 20.9% mean body weight reduction at 72 weeks (15mg). Greater than any prior anti-obesity agent.
Limitations: Cost; long-term durability unclear.
Liraglutide and Cardiovascular Outcomes in Type 2 Diabetes (LEADER) ↗
RCT n=9340 2016 NEJM
Marso et al.
Finding: 13% reduction in MACE and 22% reduction in CV mortality over 3.8 years in T2D patients with high CV risk. First clear CV-benefit signal for the GLP-1 class.
Limitations: T2D-only, high baseline CV risk — does not extrapolate directly to lower-risk populations.
Two-Year Effects of Semaglutide on Body Weight in Adults with Overweight or Obesity (STEP 5) ↗
RCT n=304 2022 Nature Medicine
Garvey et al.
Finding: 15.2% mean weight loss vs 2.6% placebo at 104 weeks — durability evidence; weight regained partially when stopped in extension data.
Limitations: Modest sample for 2-year endpoint; significant attrition typical of long obesity trials.
Effects of Once-Weekly Tirzepatide on Lean Mass: A Post Hoc Analysis of SURMOUNT-1 ↗
Post-hoc analysis of RCT n=2539 2024 Diabetes Obes Metab
Look et al.
Finding: Of total weight lost on tirzepatide ~25% was lean mass — similar proportion to caloric restriction generally. Resistance training during treatment is essential to preserve function, especially in older adults.
Limitations: Post-hoc; DEXA not gold-standard for muscle vs water; clinical implications still being studied.

Studied Dose / Protocol

Semaglutide 2.4mg/wk SC (Wegovy). Tirzepatide 5-15mg/wk SC (Zepbound).

Longevity Endpoints Evaluated

  • MACE (mortality, MI, stroke)
  • All-cause mortality (subgroup)
  • T2DM remission
  • Cardiovascular events

Side Effects

  • Nausea (often resolves)
  • Constipation/diarrhea
  • Lean mass loss (~30% of total weight)
  • Gallstones (rapid weight loss)
  • Pancreatitis (rare)
  • Gastroparesis

Contraindications / Cautions

  • Personal/family history of medullary thyroid cancer
  • MEN-2 syndrome
  • Active pancreatitis
  • Pregnancy

Low / No Alcohol

The 'small amount is good' story is dead. Less is consistently better.

Bottom Line

If you drink heavily, cutting back is high-leverage. If you drink moderately, the modern evidence does not support continuing for health. Sleep alone improves measurably. Social ritual is real, but consider lower-alcohol or non-alcoholic options.

What this means for you

The 'a glass of red wine is good for you' story is dead. Updated analyses controlling for confounders show every additional drink raises cancer risk and disrupts sleep. Less is consistently better. If you drink heavily, cutting back is one of the highest-leverage things on this site. If you drink moderately, you're not getting health benefits from continuing — but social ritual is real, so consider lower-alcohol or non-alcoholic alternatives.

How to actually do this

  • If you currently drink more than 7 (women) or 14 (men) drinks/week, cut back. Halving is a great start.
  • Set drink-free days. Aim for at least 3 alcohol-free days per week.
  • Track honestly for a month — most people underestimate by 30–50%.
  • Replace with sparkling water, NA beer (most are now genuinely good), kombucha, or tea in social settings.
  • If you crave it daily, can't stop after 1–2, or feel withdrawal symptoms when you skip — talk to your doctor. This is treatable; willpower alone isn't usually enough.
  • Best sleep night is a 0-drink night. Notice how you feel in the morning after a sober evening.
  • Pregnancy: zero alcohol.
Rough cost: Net savings. Reducing alcohol intake almost always saves money.

Worth considering if

Anyone who drinks more than 1–2 standard drinks most days. Anyone with poor sleep, hypertension, atrial fibrillation, or family history of breast/GI cancers.

Probably skip if

If you don't drink, don't start. People in recovery — protect that fully.

Helps with

Cancer Heart disease Cognitive decline Other neurodegenerative

Why it works

Ethanol breaks down into acetaldehyde — a known carcinogen that damages DNA. Even small amounts disrupt the deep-sleep portion of your night (REM goes too), bump your blood pressure for a day, and incrementally raise risk for several cancers (breast, colorectal, oral, esophageal, liver). The earlier 'J-curve' showing moderate drinkers lived longer than abstainers turned out to be confounded — many 'abstainers' had quit because of illness or were former heavy drinkers.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Ethanol is a Group 1 carcinogen. Acetaldehyde damages DNA. Daily alcohol drives BP, atrial fibrillation, breast/colorectal/oral cancers. Earlier observational 'J-curve' findings now considered confounded.

Evidence Summary

Mendelian randomization studies (Millwood Lancet 2019) and improved cohort analyses with proper reference groups show no safe level of alcohol for cancer, and no protective effect for CV mortality at any dose. Burton & Sheron (Lancet 2018) summarized the GBD 2016: alcohol's level for minimizing harm = zero. Modest CV-specific benefit at <1 drink/day disappears when ex-drinkers are properly classified.

GlyNAC (Glycine + N-Acetylcysteine)

Glutathione-restoring combo with surprisingly strong human pilot data in older adults.

Bottom Line

If even half the Sekhar effects replicate, GlyNAC is one of the most powerful single interventions on this list. Cheap, generally safe, multiple plausible mechanisms. Take with caution about over-extrapolating from one lab.

What this means for you

GlyNAC is a simple combination of two amino acids — glycine and N-acetylcysteine — that, in pilot studies in older adults, dramatically restored glutathione levels and improved many aging markers (insulin sensitivity, inflammation, cognitive function, walking speed) in just 16 weeks. The effects are so striking they need replication, but the rationale is solid and the cost is low. Among the more interesting underrated supplements on this site.

How to actually do this

  • Dose used in trials: glycine 100 mg/kg/day + NAC 100 mg/kg/day. For a 70 kg adult: ~7 g glycine + ~7 g NAC daily, split across meals.
  • Glycine is sweet-tasting and dissolves in water/coffee easily. NAC has a slight sulfur taste.
  • Start lower (3 g of each) for the first week to gauge GI tolerance, then build up.
  • Take with food. Split into 2–3 doses per day.
  • If you have asthma and use a sulfa-containing inhaler, NAC interaction is theoretical but possible — talk to your doctor.
  • If you have active peptic ulcer disease, hold off until healed — high-dose amino acids can aggravate.
  • Reasonable to try for 12–16 weeks with before/after bloodwork (CRP, fasting glucose, HbA1c). If meaningful improvements show up, continue.
Rough cost: $30–$60/month for both amino acids in bulk powder.

Worth considering if

Adults over 50 willing to bet on a high-mechanism, lower-evidence intervention. Strongest theoretical case for those with metabolic syndrome features.

Probably skip if

Active peptic ulcer. Asthmatics on certain inhalers (consult MD). Pregnancy (no data).

Why it works

Glutathione is your body's main internal antioxidant — every cell makes it. To make it, your cells need three building blocks: glycine, cysteine, and glutamic acid. Older adults are typically low on glycine and cysteine, so glutathione production drops. Just providing those two precursors lets cells rebuild glutathione — and the downstream effects on mitochondrial function, inflammation, and oxidative damage cascade outward. Sekhar's lab at Baylor has run several pilot trials with surprisingly large effects; we're waiting for larger replication.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Glycine + cysteine (from NAC) are the rate-limiting precursors for glutathione, the body's main intracellular antioxidant. Glutathione drops with age. Combo restores levels and corrects multiple downstream aging markers.

Evidence Summary

Sekhar lab at Baylor showed in elderly humans: GlyNAC 100mg/kg glycine + 100mg/kg NAC for 16 weeks improved glutathione, mitochondrial function, insulin resistance, walking speed, strength, cognition, and waist circumference. Most comprehensive single-supplement effect ever shown in older adults — but small N. Replication and longer trials needed. The dose is high (often 8-10g of each per day).

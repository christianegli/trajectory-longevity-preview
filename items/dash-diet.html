DASH Diet

The best-studied diet pattern for lowering blood pressure. Mediterranean-adjacent, but more explicit about sodium, potassium, and low-fat dairy.

Bottom Line

Use DASH when blood pressure is the main nutrition target. It is one of the few diets where the named pattern itself has strong RCT evidence.

What this means for you

DASH is the dietary pattern with the clearest blood-pressure trial evidence: vegetables, fruit, legumes, whole grains, nuts, low-fat dairy, low saturated fat, and lower sodium. It is less glamorous than Mediterranean diet, but if blood pressure is your problem, DASH is the nutrition protocol with the most direct RCT signal.

How to actually do this

  • Build meals around vegetables, fruit, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and low-fat dairy or equivalent calcium/protein sources.
  • Keep sodium near 1,500-2,300 mg/day if blood pressure is high.
  • Use beans, potatoes, leafy greens, yogurt, fruit, and nuts to raise potassium from food.
  • Replace butter, cheese-heavy meals, and processed meats with olive oil, fish, legumes, poultry, yogurt, and nuts.
  • Track home blood pressure for 4 weeks before judging effect.
Rough cost: Usually cost-neutral. More produce and low-fat dairy; less restaurant/processed food.

Worth considering if

Anyone with elevated blood pressure, family history of hypertension, high sodium intake, or cardiometabolic risk.

Probably skip if

People with advanced kidney disease or hyperkalemia need clinician guidance because potassium-rich foods can matter.

Helps with

Heart disease Diabetes / metabolic

Evidence Summary

The original DASH trial showed large blood-pressure reductions in adults with and without hypertension. DASH-Sodium then showed an additive effect from lowering sodium, with the biggest reductions in people with hypertension. Meta-analyses consistently find DASH lowers systolic blood pressure, LDL, and cardiovascular risk markers. It is a dietary pattern with direct clinical-trial evidence on a major longevity mediator: blood pressure.

Key Studies

No primary studies cataloged yet.

Studied Dose / Protocol

DASH pattern daily; sodium targets commonly 1,500-2,300 mg/day depending on trial arm.

Longevity Endpoints Evaluated

  • Blood pressure
  • LDL cholesterol
  • Cardiovascular risk markers

Possible Side Effects

  • More cooking/planning
  • stomach or gut adjustment if fiber rises quickly

Who Should Avoid This

  • Advanced chronic kidney disease with potassium restriction
  • Severe lactose intolerance if using dairy-heavy version

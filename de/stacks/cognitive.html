Cognitive Longevity Stack

For brain aging and dementia risk reduction.

For whom

Adults with a family history of dementia, current cognitive concerns ("my memory isn't what it was"), or anyone wanting to front-load cognitive risk reduction. Most evidence is observational + mechanistic — but the best moves are the same as for cardiovascular health.

What it replaces

Brain-supplement marketing. Lumosity. Most over-the-counter "nootropics."

Summary

The cognitive longevity story is mostly the cardiovascular story plus sleep. Vascular health → brain health. Sleep clears amyloid. Movement protects executive function. Diet matters specifically for the MIND pattern. Supplements have a real but secondary role.

If a single intervention here changed nothing else about your life, sleep regularity would still be the right top pick.

The stack

1
Sleep (7-9h, regularity, deep+REM)
T1Foundation $0 Ev A E 8 · C 8

Sleep is when the glymphatic system clears amyloid. Chronic short sleep correlates with later dementia risk. 7.5+ hours, regular timing.

2
VO2max Training
T1Foundation $0–50/mo Ev A E 9 · C 9

Cardiorespiratory fitness is the strongest single modifiable predictor of late-life cognitive function. Train it directly.

3
Zone 2 Cardio
T1Foundation $0 Ev A E 8 · C 9

The aerobic base. Sustained moderate exercise has independent cognitive benefit beyond the cardiovascular pathway.

4
Resistance Training
T1Foundation $30–80/mo Ev A E 7 · C 9

Strength training has cognitive evidence too — independent of cardio. Twice weekly.

5
Mediterranean Diet
T1Foundation Net neutral Ev A E 7 · C 9

MIND-diet variant has cohort evidence for slower cognitive decline. Leafy greens daily, berries daily, fish 2× weekly, beans 4× weekly.

6
Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)
T1Foundation $15–40/mo Ev A E 5 · C 7

Low DHA correlates with cognitive decline. EPA+DHA at 1–2g/day is reasonable insurance, especially for low-fish-eaters.

7
Creatine
T1Foundation $10–25/mo Ev A E 5 · C 9

Cognitive benefit concentrated in vegetarians and short sleepers, but real. 5g daily is the protocol.

8
Social Connection
T2Targeted Free Ev A E 8 · C 8

Loneliness is on the same risk-factor list as smoking for late-life dementia. The hardest one to schedule and the easiest to skip.

9
Low / No Alcohol
T2Targeted Net savings. Ev A E 6 · C 8

Heavy drinking accelerates brain volume loss measurably. Cutting to ≤3 drinks/week is one of the cleanest cognitive-protection moves available.

10
Oral Health
T2Targeted $30–200 Ev B E 4 · C 6

Periodontal disease has emerging links to dementia risk via P. gingivalis. Daily flossing and routine cleanings are real.

About

Why this exists

The longevity space is full of confident claims and weak evidence. This site grades both — what each thing might do, and how sure we are it does it. 98 interventions, every one with primary studies linked.

What you can do here

  • Take the quest — answer 8 short steps and get a personalised, evidence-graded stack of 3–12 actions with the reasoning behind each rank, a weekly schedule, and a one-click way to refine, share, or download it. All client-side, nothing sent anywhere.
  • Browse by concern — heart disease, cognitive decline, sarcopenia, frailty, diabetes, etc. 12 mechanisms mapped to interventions.
  • News — daily-ish posts on new studies, FDA actions, mechanism explainers, and editorial takes. Posts are cross-linked to the protocols they affect, so when a trial readout drops, it shows up automatically on the relevant intervention pages. Subscribe via RSS.

How it was built

Researched and rendered by Atlas (an AI Chief of Staff). Each entry started from canonical landmark studies (PREDIMED, ITP, Laukkanen sauna cohort, ATTAIN-2, etc.) and was scored against the rubric on the Methodology page.

Editorial policy & review

Every intervention page is reviewed by Trajectory editorial against the two-score rubric. Each entry carries a last reviewed date and a next review due date six months out, so you can see how fresh the take is before you trust it. Score changes are logged in What's new with the trigger study and the rationale.

Honesty notes

  • If a study link doesn't load, that's a real broken link — write to editorial@trajectory.care and we'll fix it.
  • Scores reflect a snapshot. Evidence will shift; entries carry a last_reviewed date and a next review due date.
  • Where evidence is weak, the score reflects that — we don't paper over it.
  • Scores can move down. When a high-quality trial contradicts a previous read, the page changes and the change is logged.

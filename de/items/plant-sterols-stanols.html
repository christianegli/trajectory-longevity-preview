Plant Sterols / Stanols

Reliably lowers LDL by about 7-10%. Useful as an ApoB add-on, but not a substitute for statins when risk is high.

Bottom Line

A narrow but real LDL tool. Think of it like psyllium’s cousin: useful if the target is LDL/ApoB, underwhelming if you expect whole-body longevity magic.

What this means for you

Plant sterols and stanols compete with cholesterol absorption in the gut. At the studied dose, they reliably lower LDL. The limitation: we have LDL endpoint evidence, not definitive hard-outcome evidence, and they do nothing for triglycerides, blood pressure, sleep, or muscle.

How to actually do this

  • Dose: 1.5-2 g/day plant sterols/stanols with meals.
  • Use for 8-12 weeks, then recheck LDL-C or ApoB.
  • Combine with soluble fiber and saturated-fat replacement for additive effect.
  • Do not use as a substitute for indicated lipid-lowering medication.
Rough cost: $10-40/month depending on fortified foods or capsules.

Worth considering if

People with mildly elevated LDL/ApoB who want a food/supplement add-on, especially alongside soluble fiber.

Probably skip if

Sitosterolemia. High ASCVD-risk people who need medication should not use this instead of statins/ezetimibe.

Helps with

Heart disease

Evidence Summary

Dozens of RCTs show 1.5-2 g/day plant sterols or stanols lower LDL-C by roughly 7-10% by reducing intestinal cholesterol absorption. The evidence is strong for LDL lowering, but hard cardiovascular endpoint trials are lacking. Best used as a low-risk adjunct when LDL/ApoB is the target.

Ashwagandha

Adaptogenic herb (Withania somnifera) with consistent small-to-moderate RCT evidence for reducing stress and cortisol. Not a longevity drug.

Bottom Line

Real evidence for reducing stress and cortisol. Effect size moderate, comparable to mild anxiolytics. Not a longevity drug — short-term symptom relief, not life extension. Worth considering if chronic stress is degrading your sleep and decision-making. Cycle off every few months pending long-term safety data.

What this means for you

An adaptogenic herb from Indian traditional medicine. Studies of about 400 people across 5 trials consistently show 300-600mg/day reduces stress scores and lowers cortisol by 14-32% over 6-12 weeks. Effect is real and moderate — bigger than most supplements but smaller than prescription anxiolytics. Important: this is a stress and sleep supplement, not a longevity drug.

How to actually do this

Standardized root extract (KSM-66 or Sensoril): 300-600 mg/day with food. Effect on stress takes 2-4 weeks. Cycle 8-12 weeks on, 4 weeks off until long-term safety data exists. Monitor for jaundice, fatigue, or upper-right-quadrant pain (rare liver toxicity).

Rough cost: $15-25/month for KSM-66 or Sensoril branded extract.

Why it works

Ashwagandha tones down your body's stress response (the HPA axis — the hormonal cascade that pumps out cortisol when you're stressed). Less cortisol means better sleep, less belly fat from chronic stress, and better thinking under pressure.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Withanolides (active compounds) modulate the HPA axis, dampening cortisol response to acute stress. They also bind GABA-A receptors (mild anxiolytic effect), modulate testosterone via the DHEA-S pathway, and have mild thyroid-stimulating activity. Cross blood-brain barrier.

Evidence Summary

Systematic review (Pratte 2014) of 5 RCTs found consistent reduction in stress and anxiety scales (PSS, HAM-A) at 300-600mg/day standardized extract over 6-12 weeks. Cortisol reductions of 14-32% in stressed individuals. Smaller RCTs suggest modest benefits for sleep, exercise performance, and male fertility (but the latter studies are smaller and industry-funded). No long-term safety data beyond ~12 weeks. Liver toxicity case reports exist but appear rare.

Key Studies

An alternative treatment for anxiety: a systematic review of human trial results reported for the Ayurvedic herb ashwagandha ↗
Systematic review (5 RCTs) n=400 2014 J Altern Complement Med
Pratte MA, Nanavati KB, Young V, Morley CP
Finding: Across 5 RCTs, ashwagandha consistently reduced anxiety and stress scales vs placebo. Effect sizes moderate (Cohen's d ~0.7).
Limitations: All trials short (≤12 weeks). Most industry-funded. Heterogeneous extracts and doses.
A standardized Withania somnifera extract significantly reduces stress-related parameters in chronically stressed humans: a double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled study ↗
RCT (60-day) n=130 2008 JANA
Auddy B, Hazra J, Mitra A, Abedon B, Ghosal S
Finding: Ashwagandha extract 125-500mg/day reduced PSS scores by 44%, serum cortisol by 26%, and improved CRP and well-being scores vs placebo.
Limitations: Industry-funded (Natreon). Single-site Indian population. Short duration.

Studied Dose / Protocol

300-600 mg/day of standardized root extract (KSM-66 or Sensoril are the studied brands).

Longevity Endpoints Evaluated

  • Perceived stress (PSS)
  • Anxiety (HAM-A)
  • Serum cortisol
  • Sleep quality

Possible Side Effects

  • stomach or gut upset (occasional)
  • Mild drowsiness
  • Rare liver toxicity reports

Who Should Avoid This

  • Pregnancy (avoid)
  • Hyperthyroidism (may further raise T4)
  • Autoimmune conditions (theoretical immune modulation)
  • Sedatives (additive sedation)

Bottom Line

Real if you actually practice. The 'I tried it for a week' dose isn't enough. Daily 10-20 min for years gets the meaningful effects. Free, scalable, low downside.

What this means for you

Meditation is real but only if you actually do it — daily, for years. The 'I tried it for a week' dose isn't enough. Sustained practice reliably lowers stress hormones, blood pressure, and inflammation markers, and is associated with slower cellular aging. Free, scalable, no downside (for most people). The hard part is showing up daily.

How to actually do this

  • Start with 10 minutes a day. Same time, same place. Boring is fine — boring works.
  • Use a guided app for the first 1–3 months: Waking Up (Sam Harris), Headspace, or Insight Timer. Calm is more sleep-focused.
  • Don't try to 'stop your thoughts.' Just notice them and return to your anchor (breath, sound, body sensation).
  • Build to 20–30 min/day over 6–12 months. The benefits scale with cumulative practice.
  • Try a 1-day silent retreat after 6 months of daily practice. A multi-day retreat after a year. These compress months of progress into days.
  • If meditation triggers severe anxiety, dissociation, or worsens trauma symptoms, stop and seek a trauma-informed teacher.
  • Walking meditation, tai chi, and mindful yoga are valid alternatives if seated meditation doesn't fit you.
Rough cost: $0–$15/month for an app. Retreats $300–$2000. The actual practice is free.

Worth considering if

Anyone with chronic stress, hypertension (low-end help), insomnia, anxiety, or who simply wants to age better. Especially valuable in midlife and beyond.

Probably skip if

Active psychosis or severe untreated PTSD without professional support. Don't dive into deep practice during acute crisis.

Helps with

Heart disease Cognitive decline Chronic inflammation

Why it works

Chronic stress keeps your nervous system stuck in fight-or-flight mode: cortisol elevated, blood pressure up, inflammation simmering. Meditation trains the part of the brain that turns the stress response down. Long-term meditators have measurable structural brain differences, slower aging of their telomeres (the protective caps on chromosomes), and lower inflammatory markers. None of this happens from a few sessions; it's a years-long practice that compounds.

For the curious — technical mechanism

Sustained meditation practice modulates HPA axis, reduces sympathetic tone, lowers BP, reduces inflammatory markers. Long-term practice associated with structural brain changes and slower telomere attrition.

Evidence Summary

Schneider et al. (TM long-term cohort, 2012) showed reduced all-cause mortality (~25%) and CV mortality in TM practitioners vs controls. Goyal meta-analysis (JAMA Intern Med 2014) found moderate evidence for anxiety, depression, pain reduction; weak evidence on substance use, mood, sleep. BP-reducing effect modest (~3-5 mmHg). Effects highly dose-dependent (regular practice over years vs occasional).

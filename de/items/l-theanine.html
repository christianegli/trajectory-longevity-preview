L-Theanine

Amino acid found in green tea. Promotes calm focus without sedation. Modest evidence for stress and sleep quality, no effect on lifespan.

Bottom Line

Cheap and safe. Real but small effect on stress/focus. Not a longevity intervention per se — won't extend your life. But might make you nicer to be around when stressed, and that's worth something. Skip if you don't drink coffee or have stress issues.

What this means for you

An amino acid in green tea that gives you 'calm focus' — the reason green tea feels different from coffee even though both have caffeine. About 200mg before stressful tasks reduces anxiety without making you sleepy. Cheap and very safe.

How to actually do this

200 mg L-theanine taken with morning coffee (the 1:2 theanine:caffeine ratio is studied) for calm focus. Or 200-400 mg an hour before bed for stress/sleep. Available as standalone capsules or paired with caffeine in many nootropic stacks.

Rough cost: $8-15/month for generic L-theanine capsules.

Worth considering if

Probably skip if

["You don't drink coffee and aren't stressed — no real upside", "You're chasing longevity per se — no mortality data, this is a quality-of-life supplement"]

Cognitive decline

Why it works

L-theanine increases your brain's alpha waves (the pattern your brain makes when you're relaxed but alert, like during meditation). It does this by adjusting the balance of two key brain signals — calming you down without making you tired.

For the curious — technical mechanism

L-theanine crosses the blood-brain barrier and modulates GABA, dopamine, and serotonin levels. Increases alpha-band EEG activity (8-12 Hz) within 30-45 min of dosing. Antagonizes glutamate at NMDA receptors (mild). When co-administered with caffeine, it dampens the over-arousal/jittery profile while preserving the alertness benefit.

Evidence Summary

Small RCTs (n=10-50) show 200-400mg theanine reduces subjective stress, improves attention performance under stress, and may modestly improve sleep quality (without sedation). Williams 2020 systematic review: 11 RCTs supportive but underpowered for definitive conclusions. No mortality or longevity-endpoint data — included here because Bryan Johnson takes it and it's commonly recommended for stress/sleep, not because it extends life.

Key Studies

L-Theanine, a natural constituent in tea, and its effect on mental state ↗
Review of human EEG and behavioral studies 2008 Asia Pac J Clin Nutr
Nobre AC, Rao A, Owen GN
Finding: L-theanine 50-200mg increases alpha brainwave activity (EEG) within 30-45 min, associated with subjective relaxation without sedation. Synergy with caffeine for attention performance.
Limitations: Review of small studies. Subjective endpoints. No long-term data.
Effects of L-theanine administration on stress-related symptoms and cognitive functions in healthy adults ↗
RCT (4-week parallel) n=30 2019 Nutrients
Hidese S, Ogawa S, Ota M, Ishida I, Yasukawa Z, Ozeki M, Kunugi H
Finding: 200mg theanine/day for 4 weeks reduced stress-related symptoms (PSQI sleep score, anxiety) and improved verbal fluency vs placebo.
Limitations: Small sample. Single industry-funded study (Taiyo Kagaku).

Studied Dose / Protocol

100-400 mg/day, often paired with caffeine (1:2 theanine:caffeine ratio for the 'calm focus' synergy).

Longevity Endpoints Evaluated

  • Sleep quality (PSQI)
  • Attention task performance

Possible Side Effects

  • Mild headache (rare)
